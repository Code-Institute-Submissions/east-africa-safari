Maasai Mara National Reserve

It is one of the key popular tourism points in Kenya, located in South-Western Kenya along the Great-Rift Valley adjacent to Serengeti National Park of Tanzania. The attractions in Maasai Mara National Reserve are both Nature and Wildlife. The Wildebeest migration is also witnessed in the Kenya iconic Maasai Mara National Reserve. A good short safari combination for those hoping to also see all these charismatic creatures (The Big Five)(and much more besides) would be Maasai Mara (for lion, leopard, bufallo, and elephant) and Lake Nakuru (for black and white rhino).

Hell's Gate National Park

It lies South of Lake Naivasha in Kenya, North West of Nairobi. A small National Park. It is known for its wide variety of wildlife and for its scenery. There is a wide variety of Wildlife in the National Park though many are few in number, example of little seen wildlife include lions, leopards, and cheetahs. However the park has historically been an important home for the rare lammergeyer vultures, Verreaux's eagles. augur buzzard, and swifts. Hyraxes, African buffalo, zebra, eland, hartebeest, Thomson's gazelle hyena and baboons are also common.

Wilderbeast Migration

The best time to witness the migration from the Serengeti to Maasai Mara is between July and September. During this period the greatest spectacle on earth unfolds right before your eyes. Over a million wildebeest, zebra and gazelles make their way to Maasai Mara by crossing the crocodile-infested Mara River in search for greener pastures. Unfortunately though many are attacked and killed in the process.

The exact timing may change from year to year as it is a spontaneous event influenced by rainfall patterns and the subsequent grazing opportunities. The animals are constantly on the move all year-round. They stay in the Maasai Mara from July to october before gradually migrating back to the Serengeti from November.
